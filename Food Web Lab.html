Lesson 3 · Energy, Producers & Consumers

Build a Food Web: Who Eats Whom?

Digital Lab — drag, connect, and classify on your laptop instead of with yarn and poster paper

Topic & objective

Topic: Herbivores, Carnivores, Omnivores

Objective: Students will construct a food web from organism cards, classify each organism as herbivore, carnivore, or omnivore, and trace energy flow.

Background

Energy flows through ecosystems from producers to consumers. Herbivores eat only plants, carnivores eat only animals, and omnivores eat both. Arrows in a food web point FROM the organism being eaten TO the organism doing the eating — showing the direction of energy flow.

Materials (digital version)

Laptop or Chromebook with this page open — no physical materials needed. The organism cards, connecting lines, and poster space are all built into the lab below.

SAFETY NOTE: No safety concerns for this digital activity — keep your laptop on a flat, stable surface.

Procedure

1. Drag each organism card from the left-hand palette onto the canvas below.

2. Click "Connect" mode, then click one organism and then the organism that eats it, to draw an arrow showing energy flow. Repeat until all feeding relationships are connected.

3. Click each organism's label to cycle through Producer / Herbivore / Carnivore / Omnivore and classify it correctly.

4. Use the "Highlight mouse crash" button to see — in a second color — every organism affected if the mouse population disappeared.

5. Fill in the Data Table using your completed food web.

6. Answer the Analysis Questions at the bottom of the page.

Analysis questions

1. What would happen to the hawk population if the mouse population disappeared? Trace the effect through the web.
2. Why do food webs have more producers than top-level carnivores?
3. Is a bear an herbivore, carnivore, or omnivore? How does its classification affect its position on the food web?
